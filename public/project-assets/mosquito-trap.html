Assembly steps

1

Mount fans to PVC reducer mechanical

Stack two 120mm fans at the narrow (75mm) end of the PVC reducer, both oriented to pull air inward through the funnel. Secure with zip ties or M4 bolts through the fan frame corners. Use a rubber gasket or silicone sealant to close the gap and maximize suction.

2

Attach catch bag mechanical

Tie the nylon mesh bag to the fan outlet side (downstream of the fan). Hang loosely — don't pack it tight or airflow resistance drops and suction weakens. A cable tie around the fan frame works.

3

Install CO₂ nozzle at funnel rim CO₂

Drill a 4mm hole in the wide rim of the PVC reducer. Push airline tubing through and angle the end ~30–45 degrees outward and downward — pointing away from the funnel center. Seal around tubing with silicone.

4

Add lactic acid wick attractant

Fill a small 10–20ml glass vial halfway with lactic acid. Place a cotton wick with exposed end outside the vial. Mount just inside the funnel mouth with a zip tie or wire. Replace every 2–3 weeks.

5

Connect CO₂ line CO₂

Regulator screws onto cylinder valve. Bubble counter goes inline between regulator output and the airline tubing. Check all connections with soapy water. Set needle valve to ~1 bubble per second, which approximates 0.5 kg/day flow rate.

6

Wire and power up electrical

Connect both fans in parallel to the 12V/2A+ adapter. Run cable to outdoor outlet. Confirm both fans spin and you can feel suction at the wide funnel mouth. Done.

Placement & operation

📍 Placement

✓

BBQ area corner, where two stone walls meet — maximizes wind protection and rain cover

✓

Funnel mouth facing outward toward garden and vegetation

✓

CO₂ cylinder on far side from BBQ pit

✓

Shaded position preferred — Aedes rest near cool shaded spots

⚠

Turn off during active grilling. Back on after.

⏱ Operation schedule

✓

First 4–6 weeks: run 24/7 to break the breeding cycle

✓

After population drops: peak hours only — 5–9am and 5–9pm — saves ~65% CO₂

✓

Empty catch bag every 2–3 days — tie closed, dispose, replace

✓

Replace lactic acid wick every 2–3 weeks

✓

Check bubble counter daily — no bubbles means check connections or cylinder level

What it catches

Aedes aegypti

Dengue / Zika / Chikungunya vector. Primary target. Highly responsive to CO₂ + lactic acid.

Maruins (midges)

Extremely CO₂-responsive. Likely second-highest catch. Significant nuisance reduction.

Pigeon flies

Hippoboscid ectoparasites. Catchable when dispersing. Benefit for wildlife rehab operation.

Mutucas (horse flies)

CO₂-responsive biting flies. Moderate catch rate.

Moths / beetles

Incidental only. No UV = not actively attracted. Low bycatch.

Bees / butterflies

Not host-seeking. Zero bycatch. Safe for wildlife.
